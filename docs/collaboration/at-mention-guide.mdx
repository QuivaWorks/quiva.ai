# @Mention Guide – Adding Team Members to Sessions

The @mention feature is the primary way to invite team members to collaborative sessions. You can also use @mentions to control whether you're notifying your team members or the AI assistant itself.

## How @Mentions Work

### Basic @Mention Syntax

Type `@` in the chat prompt to open the mention picker:
```
@[team member name]
```

**Example:**
- `@Sarah, can you review this?`
- `@Jake and @Maria, what do you think?`
- `@team, let's get input on this`

### The @Mention Picker

When you type `@`, a dropdown appears showing:

- **Team members** – Available people to add to the session
- **Search** – Type a name to filter the list
- **Click to select** – Click a name to add them

![@Mention Picker](image-placeholder-mention-picker.png)

Once selected, the teammate's name appears as a highlighted mention in your message.

## AI vs. Team Notifications

When you use @mentions, you have two choices:

### **@Mention Team Members Only**

Invite teammates to the conversation without instructing the AI.
```
@Sarah, @Jake – I need your thoughts on this approach before we proceed
```

**What happens:**
- Sarah and Jake are added to the session
- They receive notifications
- The AI doesn't see the mention as a special instruction
- Use this when you want team input, not AI action

### **@Mention the AI Assistant**

Instruct the AI while adding team members.
```
@Assistant: @Sarah – please review this code and provide feedback
```

**What happens:**
- Sarah is added to the session
- The AI receives an instruction to review the code
- Sarah sees the AI's response in real-time
- Use this when you want the AI to perform a task AND team visibility

### **How to Choose**

| Scenario | Use |
|----------|-----|
| Want team input on a topic | @mention team members only |
| Want AI to perform a task | @mention the AI |
| Want AI to review for a team member | @mention both |
| Just having a conversation | Don't use @mentions |

**Tip:** The system will prompt you to clarify if ambiguous. You'll see: "Did you want to notify [teammates] or the AI?"

## @Mention Best Practices

### Do's ✅

- **Be specific** – `@Sarah – can you check the metrics?` is better than just `@Sarah`
- **Use for important items** – Reserve @mentions for things that need immediate attention
- **Include context** – Explain why you're looping in teammates
- **One per action** – Don't spam mentions in a single message

### Don'ts ❌

- **Don't mention everyone** – Only include relevant team members
- **Don't over-use** – Frequent mentions reduce their impact
- **Don't use for casual chat** – Not every message needs a mention
- **Don't mention inactive users** – They may not see the notification

## Examples

### Example 1: Requesting Team Review
```
@Sarah, @Mike – can you both review the data analysis below?
I want to make sure we're not missing anything before the client call.

[analysis here]
```

**What happens:**
- Sarah and Mike are added to the session
- They both receive notifications
- They can ask follow-up questions
- You all see the conversation in one place

### Example 2: Delegating to AI with Team Visibility
```
@Assistant: create a summary of the Q3 financial report.

@Sarah – need this for the investor presentation tomorrow

```

**What happens:**
- Sarah is added to the session
- The AI creates the summary
- Sarah sees it immediately and can request changes
- No need to forward files or recreate context

### Example 3: Collaborative Problem Solving
```
We're stuck on the authentication bug.

@Assistant: help us debug this error
@Jake, @Elena – you've both worked on auth before, thoughts?
```

**What happens:**
- Jake and Elena are added to the session
- The AI helps debug
- Teammates can contribute their experience
- Full collaboration happens in one thread

## Troubleshooting

### Can't find a team member in @mentions?

- **They're not in your workspace** – Check your Account Management → User Management
- **They're deactivated** – Only active team members appear in @mentions
- **Spelling matters** – Make sure you have the correct username

### @Mention not working?

- **Check permissions** – Ensure you have permission to add team members to sessions
- **Browser cache** – Refresh the page and try again
- **Session type** – You can only @mention in team-visible sessions (check the lock icon)

### Team member says they didn't get notified

- **Check notification settings** – They may have disabled notifications for shared sessions
- **They already had access** – If they already had session access, there's no new notification
- **Check "Shared With Me"** – The session should appear there even without a notification
