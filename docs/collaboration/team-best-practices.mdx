# Team Collaboration Best Practices

Get the most out of collaborative sessions with these proven strategies and workflows.

## Workflow Patterns

### Pattern 1: Real-Time Brainstorming

**Best for:** Ideation, problem-solving, creative tasks

**Workflow:**
1. Start a session with the AI assistant
2. Ask an initial question
3. @mention relevant team members
4. All members contribute ideas in real-time
5. The AI helps synthesize and expand ideas

**Tips:**
- Keep sessions to 3-5 people to avoid chaos
- Assign one person to guide the conversation
- Use the AI to organize and summarize contributions

### Pattern 2: Delegated Review

**Best for:** Code reviews, content reviews, quality checks

**Workflow:**
1. You ask the AI to review something
2. @mention subject matter experts
3. Each expert sees the AI's analysis
4. They provide feedback in the same session
5. Iterate based on all feedback at once

**Tips:**
- Be specific about what needs review
- Include context for reviewers
- Set clear feedback deadlines

### Pattern 3: Async Task Execution

**Best for:** Reports, analysis, writing tasks

**Workflow:**
1. Create a session with the AI
2. @mention teammates who need to see results
3. You work async – teammates check when they can
4. Unread badges keep them informed
5. They ask follow-up questions without context switching

**Tips:**
- Share the session upfront, not the final result
- Let teammates see the working process
- Encourage inline feedback

### Pattern 4: Knowledge Capture

**Best for:** Learning, documentation, training

**Workflow:**
1. Experts collaborate with the AI on a topic
2. @mention team members who need to learn
3. The AI generates training materials in real-time
4. Teammates can ask clarifying questions
5. Full conversation becomes knowledge base

**Tips:**
- Record the conversation for reference
- Have the AI create summaries as you go
- Pause to answer teammate questions

## Communication Strategies

### Be Clear About Intent

When @mentioning, be explicit:

❌ **Unclear:** `@Sarah – what do you think?`

✅ **Clear:** `@Sarah – we're considering using React for this. Given your experience with Vue, what are the trade-offs?`

### Provide Context Upfront

Don't assume teammates have the backstory:

❌ **Missing context:** `@Jake, review this?`

✅ **With context:** `@Jake, we're optimizing the database query for the new dashboard. The current version takes 10s to load. Can you review the approach?`

### Respect Async Boundaries

Not everyone can respond immediately:

- ✅ Share sessions expecting async responses
- ✅ Mute notifications so people can focus
- ✅ Don't expect immediate replies
- ❌ Don't ping teammates constantly about shared sessions

### Summarize Decisions

When a team reaches consensus:

1. Summarize the decision in the session
2. Ask the AI to document key points
3. Share the summary with non-participants
4. Close the session with final status

## Session Management

### Naming Conventions

Use clear, descriptive session names:

| ❌ Bad | ✅ Good |
|---------|---------|
| `Chat with AI` | `Q3 Budget Review – Finance Team` |
| `Work Session 3` | `Customer Churn Analysis – Data Team` |
| `Collab` | `Content Review: Blog Series – Marketing` |

### Participant Limits

**Ideal team size:** 2-5 people

- **2-3 people** – Quick decisions, focused work
- **4-5 people** – Complex problems, diverse input
- **6+ people** – Better as separate sessions with summary sharing

### Session Duration

- **1-2 hours** – Best for active collaboration
- **4+ hours** – Consider splitting into multiple sessions
- **Days/weeks** – Archive when decision is made

## Performance Optimization

### Keep Sessions Focused

**Do:**
- ✅ One topic per session
- ✅ Define the goal upfront
- ✅ End the session when complete
- ✅ Archive after decision

**Don't:**
- ❌ Use one session for multiple unrelated topics
- ❌ Leave sessions open indefinitely
- ❌ Add people who aren't relevant

### Manage Notifications

**To reduce noise:**
- Mute less urgent sessions
- Disable notifications during focus time
- Check Shared With Me once per hour instead
- Turn off sound for non-critical sessions

## Handling Conflicts

### When Disagreement Happens

1. **Keep it in the session** – Don't take offline unnecessarily
2. **Ask the AI for options** – Neutral analysis helps
3. **Let everyone contribute** – Hear all perspectives
4. **Document the decision** – Why you chose one path
5. **Move forward** – Commit to the decision

### When Someone Misunderstands

1. **Clarify kindly** – Misunderstandings happen
2. **Use the AI to explain** – Fresh perspective helps
3. **Provide examples** – Concrete examples > abstract concepts
4. **Summarize** – Confirm understanding before moving on

## Privacy & Security

### What to Share

- ✅ Work-related content
- ✅ Analytical data
- ✅ Code reviews
- ✅ Strategy discussions

### What NOT to Share

- ❌ Sensitive customer data (PII)
- ❌ Financial passwords or tokens
- ❌ Personal information
- ❌ Confidential business secrets

**Tip:** Use the **lock icon** to keep sensitive sessions private.

## Example Workflows

### Workflow: New Feature Spec

**Goal:** Align team on new feature specification

**Steps:**
1. Start session: "Payment System Redesign Spec"
2. Write initial requirements
3. @mention frontend lead, backend lead, PM
4. Each provides constraints and technical input
5. AI synthesizes into comprehensive spec
6. Document final spec in session summary
7. Archive and reference as source of truth

**Time:** 30-60 minutes  
**Participants:** 4 people

### Workflow: Data Analysis Review

**Goal:** Validate analysis and presentation

**Steps:**
1. Start session: "Q3 Marketing Analysis"
2. Share raw data and initial analysis
3. @mention data analyst and marketing director
4. Analyst validates methodology
5. Director suggests presentation improvements
6. AI creates final report
7. Both sign off in session

**Time:** 45-90 minutes  
**Participants:** 2-3 people

### Workflow: Project Kickoff

**Goal:** Align entire team before starting

**Steps:**
1. Create session: "Project X – Team Kickoff"
2. Outline scope and timeline
3. @mention all core team members
4. Each shares their role and concerns
5. AI documents decisions and action items
6. Confirm everyone understands
7. Session becomes kickoff record

**Time:** 1-2 hours  
**Participants:** 5-8 people

## Metrics & Health Checks

### Signs Your Collaboration is Working

- ✅ Decisions happen faster
- ✅ Fewer follow-up meetings needed
- ✅ Team feels heard and informed
- ✅ Context is preserved in sessions
- ✅ Async work is productive

### Red Flags

- ❌ Sessions are too large (6+ people)
- ❌ Conversations drag on (4+ hours)
- ❌ People are ignored or not responding
- ❌ Decisions aren't being documented
- ❌ Team avoids using shared sessions
